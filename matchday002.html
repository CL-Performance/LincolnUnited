Lincoln Soccer Club vs Lincoln United

By SAM LAUGHLIN        June 10th, 2017


Coming off a 1-0 home victory against Indy Saints FC, Lincoln United spent the week in preparation at their training facility, Doane University, before making the short journey across the city to take on Lincoln Soccer Club in the first instalment of the Lancaster County Derby.


United’s first meeting against local rivals, LSC, was a dramatic one as both sides looked to build off their performance against the same opposition last weekend. United’s traditional style of play was put to the test early, as LSC brought a physicality on and off the ball.


“[Lincoln Soccer Club] are a really athletic side.” Lincoln United Manager, Chris Lawson, said. “They press high and play direct. During the early stages of the match, they were able to stretch us out by using their athleticism and pace to win long balls and get on the end of them.”

Early on, LSC were able to get in behind and earn a corner kick. On the subsequent corner, a foul was given, and LSC were awarded a penalty kick. In the 14th minute LSC forward, Job Yapp, slotted home the opener from the spot.

After conceding, United were able to regain their composure and push for an equalizer, and in the 34th minute Lamine Doumbia found the head of Marcos Soma with a tremendous cross. The score line remained level through the remainder of the first half.

The physicality of the match led to a few half chances early on in the second half, but things began to open up when Lincoln United forward, Conor McFadden, was sent off in the 61st minute.


14 minutes later, United midfielder, Daniel Maganda sprung free behind the LSC defense before being brought down by a crunching tackle from LSC defender Matthew Cotton. Cotton, having already been booked in the first half, was shown his second yellow card and both sides finished off the remaining fifteen minutes of the match with 10 men.


“You have to work harder [when you go down a man],” Lawson said. “That’s what we did, and for the fourteen minutes we had to play down, we were the better side. Once they went down to ten as well, we all knew that it was only a matter of time until we found the winner.”

In the 79th minute, United grabbed that winner when substitute Olayemi Olaigbe played a ball over the heads of the LSC defense and into the path of a well-timed Lamine Doubmia run.

Doumbia was able to bring the ball under control and slide it underneath the advancing keeper. United were able to see out the three points in a manner that the manager called both a spirited and professional performance.

Sometimes local matches can be overhyped or dubbed as rivalries prematurely, leading to boring, uneventful matches. However this was not the case on Saturday, and even though it was only the first Lancaster County Derby, it didn’t lack the passion and intensity established rivalries tend to have.


“They were ready for us,” Doumbia said. “But we responded very well in the second half.”


It was a match that will certainly leave the fans ready for the reverse fixture, but by earning three points away on Saturday, United move to second in Region II of the CSL; only behind South Bend FC on goal differential.


United look to continue their winning ways when they travel to Indiana this weekend to take on Indy Saints and South Bend FC. Results will be of the utmost importance as United looks towards first in the league.
