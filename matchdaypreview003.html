Lancaster County Derby Preview

By SAM LAUGHLIN        July 7th, 2017


After a two week hiatus from league competition, Lincoln United is primed to resume their 2017 league campaign at home against local rivals Lincoln Soccer Club on Saturday, June 8 at 5 pm. The match will be hosted at Doane University’s Al Papik Stadium.


The reverse fixture against LSC finished with United edging out an away result with a 2-1 win in dramatic fashion. United Manager, Chris Lawson, expects that Saturday's match could provide more drama as both clubs compete for league positions.

“Lincoln Soccer Club isn’t an easy side to face,” Lawson said. “They’re an athletic team and they have a few threats that can punish you if you’re not switched on.”

Despite currently sitting on the lower end of the table, LSC were able to get results against both Indy Saints and South Bend FC, as well as make life difficult for United in the first Lancaster County Derby. 


With the drama of the previous fixture still looming in the air, it will be important for both sides to stay emotionally under control and focused on the task at hand if they want to secure three points on Saturday.


“We obviously got off to a slow start in our previous fixture against them [Lincoln Soccer Club].” Lawson said. “With our two-week break from league competition, the most important aspect to our success this weekend is going to be our mental preparation and focus. The key to winning derbies is all about coming out on the front foot, so that’s what we’ve been pushing towards these last two weeks.” 


Since their last league match, United have travelled to Des Moines to face stiff competition in the Des Moines Menace PDL side, as well as hosting a closed-doors friendly. 


The teams appear to be evenly matched heading into Saturday, as the weekend approaches, it looks like the preparation from both sides is drawing to a close. 


“At this point there isn’t much more preparation we can do.” Lawson Said. “We have the advantage playing at home, but like most derbies, I think the match is going to be decided by who wants it more.”


Tickets will be available for purchase once the gates open at 4:00pm. To stay up to date on the second installment of the Lancaster County Derby, or for all other Lincoln United news and updates: Follow us on twitter @LincolnUnitedNE
